Welcome to Madrid!

In Madrid, Syracuse University students will find a unique and enriching academic experience. One of Spain's most historic cities, Madrid is home to a wide range of artistic displays and resources. Students can take courses at Syracuse University's Madrid Center or attend classes taught completely in Spanish at the Universidad Autonoma de Madrid. During their time in Madrid, students will become more proficient in reading, writing and speaking Spanish. They can also take part in university-hosted cultural activities or service learning program to become further engaged in Madrid's rich culture.

Attractions

Madrid's rich artistic and cultural history are on display through its countless landmarks and attractions. The "MadWorld Passport program" also can help students further explore the city by encouraging them to participate in cultural events for monetary rewards. Between classes, students are encouraged to venture out of the classroom and visit Madrid's museums, wander down the city's vibrant streets, and sample the local cuisine at centuries-old restaurants.

Courses

A wide range of courses in a variety of areas of study are available for students spending the semester in Madrid. Courses are taught in both Englsh and Spanish at the Madrid Center and at the Universidad Autonoma de Madrid. Courses are available for students at all levels of Spanish proficiency. Students interested in studying Spanish, History, or other areas of the humanities have dozens of possible courses to choose from. Housing & Pricing

Students studying in Madrid can live with a Spanish family for a homestay or reside in a residence hall. In a homestay experience, students are fully immersed in Spanish culture and have the opportunity to experience traditional meals, customs and celebrations. Students can also live in the Colegio Mayor El Faro residence hall, which is located in downtown Madrid. In this residence hall, you will live with local and other international students and be randomly assigned a roommate.

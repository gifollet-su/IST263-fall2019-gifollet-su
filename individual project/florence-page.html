Welcome to Florence!

Studying abroad in Florence, Italy offers Syracuse University students the opportunity to surround themselves with a rich artistic and cultural history. Students will expand their proficiency in the Italian language while taking courses at Syracuse University's Florence Center or at the University of Florence. Outside of their academic pursuits, students are urged to leave campus and become familiar with the rich cultural opportunities both within the city and throughout the country of Italy. Students can also take part in an intership to gain professional experience in an international setting. Whatever area of study students choose to pursue, Florence offers a deeply engaging and educational academic experience.

Attractions

Florence is home to stunning architecture, delicious local cuisine and dozens of rich art sources. International students from dozens of global universities converge in the city, and there are countless educational and cultural learning experiences available to explore. Students are also able to take part in site visits and cultural field trips throughout Italy arranged by Syracuse University to become more familiar with the area's unique culture.


Notable attractions:

  • Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore
  • Piazza Duomo
  • Piazzale Michelangiolo
  • Uffizi Palace and Gallery
  • Piazza della Signoria/Loggia dei Lanzi
  • Gallera dell'Accademia

Courses

As a hub of creative pursuits, Syracuse University's Florence Center offers dozens of courses in artistic areas such as Architecure, Art History and Design. Students can also increase their Italian proficiency or study literature, nutrition, political science or many additional areas of study. At the University of Florence, students can take classes in economics, mathematics, international relations and political science. Internships are also offered for students who want to gain further professional experience in their field or take advantage of the opportunity to work for an international organization. Housing & Pricing

While studying in Florence, the majority of students will live with a host family. Many students find living with an Italian family to be a valuable experience and an opportunity to both improve their Italian proficiency and gain first-hand experience with authentic Italian traditions, cuisine and celebrations. These families are thoroughly screened by Syracuse University to ensure that they pose no threat to students, and can range from large families to elderly couples to couples with young children. Architecture and studio arts students studying in Florence can choose to share an apartment with other students if they prefer not to live with a host family.
